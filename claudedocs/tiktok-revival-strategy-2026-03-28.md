## 1. SHOULD YOU STILL INVEST IN TIKTOK?

**Short answer**: Yes, but radically differently. Not more content — different content, less often.

**The case FOR keeping TikTok**:
- TikTok engagement rate in 2026 is 3.70% average (7x Instagram's 0.48%). Even a suppressed TikTok account has higher ceiling potential per video than IG.
- TikTok's algorithm is format-blind for small creators — a 3K follower account can hit 500K views on a single video. Instagram increasingly rewards existing audience size.
- Your 63% female TikTok audience is actually your ideal Givore user (women 25-44 driving household decisions about furniture).
- The "segunda mano" keyword cluster has 670K combined monthly searches across Spanish cities — there IS massive demand for this topic.

**The case AGAINST over-investing**:
- Instagram is your proven performer (+91% followers, 295K impressions, stable growth). It would be strategically reckless to take resources away from what works.
- Your TikTok account shows classic shadowban signals: sudden view drop from thousands to ~200, zero new followers for an entire month, views not recovering even after resuming posting.
- Zero-budget means you cannot split attention equally across platforms.

**Verdict: 70/20/10 split**.
- 70% effort on Instagram (your growth engine, proven audience, best demographics)
- 20% effort on TikTok (experimental lab — test formats here, repurpose winners to IG)
- 10% on Facebook/YouTube (crosspost only, no original effort)

This means: 1-2 TikTok originals per week maximum, not 7. Quality over volume is the 2026 algorithm reality — one genuinely engaging video per week outperforms seven mediocre daily posts.

## 2. DIAGNOSING THE TIKTOK COLLAPSE

Your TikTok decline is NOT random. Here is what happened, based on the data:

### Problem 1: Content Format Monotony
You posted the same cycling POV format 7+ times per week. TikTok's 2026 algorithm tracks "content diversity signals" — when every video has identical structure (same hook pattern, same visual format, same narrative arc), the algorithm classifies your content as repetitive and reduces distribution. This is not a shadowban from rule violations — it is algorithmic boredom.

### Problem 2: The Posting Gap Penalty
The week-long break in late Feb/early March compounded the problem. TikTok's algorithm rewards consistency signals, and a sudden stop after daily posting tells the algorithm your account is unreliable. When you resumed, you returned to the same format, giving the algorithm zero reason to redistribute.

### Problem 3: Completion Rate Collapse
Cycling POV videos with AI-narrated scripts likely have poor completion rates after the audience has seen the format 50+ times. In 2026, completion rate and shares are the two metrics that matter most (likes count less than before). 73% of viral videos have completion rates above 80%. If viewers are swiping away at second 5 because they know exactly what the next 55 seconds look like, TikTok stops showing your content.

### Problem 4: No Share Trigger
"I cycled through Ruzafa and found a chair" is informative but not shareable. Your top IG reel ("Un archivador de oficina" at 58K views) worked because it had a SPECIFIC OBJECT with a STORY. Generic neighborhood cycling has no "I need to send this to Maria" moment.

## 3. TIKTOK ACCOUNT RECOVERY PLAN

### Week 1-2: The Reset (Do This Immediately)

**Step 1: Stop posting the cycling POV format on TikTok entirely for 14 days.**
This is counterintuitive but necessary. Shadowban recovery research consistently shows that 2-4 weeks of reduced or paused posting helps the algorithm reset. During this time:
- Go through old comment sections and reply to every unanswered comment
- Engage authentically with 10-15 Spanish creators in adjacent niches daily (upcycling, Valencia lifestyle, minimalism, cycling)
- Do NOT delete any existing videos (the algorithm penalizes deletions)

**Step 2: During the pause, create 5-7 videos in completely NEW formats** (see Section 4 below). Bank them. Do not post yet.

**Step 3: Relaunch with a new posting rhythm.**
- 3 posts per week maximum (Tue/Thu/Sat, 12:00-14:00 and 19:00-21:00 CET — peak Spanish TikTok hours)
- Each post must be a DIFFERENT format from the other two that week
- No two consecutive posts should look visually similar

### Week 3-4: Test and Measure
- Post the banked content, one format at a time
- Track: completion rate (>50% = good), share rate (>1% = viral potential), comments (quality, not quantity)
- Double down on whichever format gets the best completion rate, NOT the most views

### Week 5+: Establish New Rhythm
- Settle on 2-3 winning formats
- Rotate between them so no format appears more than twice per week
- Reserve cycling POV for maximum 1 video per week, and only when something genuinely remarkable happens during the ride

## 4. SPECIFIC CONTENT FORMATS THAT WOULD WORK

Here are 8 formats designed for this exact niche. None of them are "cycling POV with narration."

### Format A: "Esto Estaba En La Calle" (Object Stories)
**Why it works**: Your best-performing content already proves this. Specific objects with stories outperform generic rides 10-20x.
**Structure**:
- Hook (0-3s): Close-up of the object, text overlay: "Alguien tiró esto en [barrio]"
- Reveal (3-8s): Pull back to show full object, context of where it was found
- Story (8-20s): What is it, what condition, who might want it
- Resolution (20-30s): "Publicado en Givore. En 2 horas alguien vino a recogerlo."
**Key difference from current format**: START with the object, not the ride. The bike is incidental, the find is the content.
**Posting frequency**: 1-2x per week
**Share trigger**: "Mira lo que alguien tiró" — people share disbelief/outrage/desire

### Format B: "Lo Que Valencia Tira En Una Semana" (Weekly Compilation)
**Why it works**: Compilation/listicle format has high completion rates because viewers want to see what's next.
**Structure**:
- Hook: "Todo lo que encontré esta semana en las calles de Valencia"
- Quick cuts: 3-5 seconds per item, numbered (#1, #2... #7)
- Ranking: Save the best find for last
- Close: Total value estimate + "Todo gratis. Todo en Givore."
**Posting frequency**: 1x per week (Saturday — recap energy)
**Share trigger**: The escalation creates anticipation; viewers share the "best" item

### Format C: "Antes y Despues" (Before/After Transformations)
**Why it works**: Transformation content is one of 2026's dominant TikTok formats. "Before and after" transitions using quick movements synced to trending sounds are pulling massive engagement right now.
**Structure**:
- Frame 1: Sad item on the street, rain, ugly lighting
- Transition: Snap/wipe/movement synced to trending audio
- Frame 2: Same item cleaned up, in someone's home, looking great
- Text: "De la calle de [barrio] al salon de [name]"
**Posting frequency**: 1x per week
**Share trigger**: Satisfying visual payoff — the most save-worthy format
**Note**: This requires actually following up with people who claim items through Givore. Even 1-2 success stories per month is enough content.

### Format D: "POV: Eres Un Mueble En Valencia" (Object POV)
**Why it works**: POV format remains strong in 2026 Spain. Anthropomorphizing the discarded object is unexpected and emotionally engaging.
**Structure**:
- Camera at object level on the sidewalk
- Text narration from the object's perspective: "Llevo 3 dias aqui. Mi duena se mudo y me dejo. Nadie me mira."
- Someone opens Givore, sees the listing
- Object gets picked up: "Tengo nueva casa"
**Posting frequency**: 1x every 2 weeks
**Share trigger**: Emotional + humorous. Self-aware humor about being a brand.
**Risk**: Could feel cringe if overdone. Use sparingly and with genuine wit.

### Format E: "Cuanto Cuesta Esto Nuevo?" (Price Reveal)
**Why it works**: Price/money content consistently performs on TikTok. The gap between "free on the street" and "retail price" is inherently shocking.
**Structure**:
- Show item found on street
- Google the exact model/brand
- Reveal retail price with dramatic zoom + sound effect
- "Gratis. En la calle de [barrio]. Publicado en Givore."
**Posting frequency**: 1x per week
**Share trigger**: Financial shock value — "you won't believe what someone threw away"

### Format F: "La Ruta De Los Muebles" (Treasure Map)
**Why it works**: Gamification + local knowledge. Creates the feeling of insider information.
**Structure**:
- Show a Google Maps screenshot of Valencia
- Mark spots where items were found this week with pins
- "Los barrios donde mas se tira: 1. Eixample, 2. Ruzafa, 3. Benimaclet"
- Quick montage of finds from each neighborhood
**Posting frequency**: 1x every 2 weeks
**Share trigger**: Utility — people save and share useful local information
**Bonus**: Adapts easily to every Spanish city as Givore expands

### Format G: "Reaccion" (Reaction to Givore Posts)
**Why it works**: Reaction format is native TikTok. Uses Givore app screenshots as content.
**Structure**:
- Screen recording scrolling through Givore app listings
- Creator reacts to interesting posts: "Esto? ESTO lo tiraron?!"
- Mix of genuine surprise, humor, and commentary
- End with: "Si quereis ver lo que la gente tira en vuestra zona..."
**Posting frequency**: 1x per week
**Share trigger**: The app itself becomes content. Every reaction is also a product demo without being an ad.

### Format H: "El Vecino Que..." (Community Stories)
**Why it works**: Storytelling/community content aligns with 2026's "slow content" trend. Real human stories beat production value.
**Structure**:
- Face to camera, conversational tone
- "Hoy un senor de 70 anos me escribio por Givore. Queria regalar el escritorio de su hijo que se fue a trabajar a Berlin..."
- Simple story, real emotion
- No hard CTA — the story IS the marketing
**Posting frequency**: 1x every 2 weeks (only when you have a real story)
**Share trigger**: Emotional resonance. Real stories get shared to friends with "mira que bonito"

## 6. TRENDING FORMATS IN SPAIN — MARCH/APRIL 2026

Based on current research, here are specific trends you can adapt:

### Trend 1: "Slow Content" / Substance Over Speed
Spain's TikTok is shifting away from rapid-fire edits toward longer, more substantive videos. This actually favors your niche — a 45-60 second story about a specific furniture find has more value than a 15-second cycling montage. Lean into this with Formats A, E, and H above.

### Trend 2: Spring Cleaning Transformations
March-April is peak decluttering season in Spain (mudanzas season). The "spring cleaning transformation" format is trending hard:
- "Todo lo que saque de mi piso este fin de semana"
- "Mi mudanza minimalista — regalo todo lo que no necesito"
- This is DIRECTLY your niche. Create content targeted at people GIVING AWAY, not just finding.

### Trend 3: "Romanticize Your Life" Montages
The aesthetic lifestyle montage format remains strong. Adapt it:
- "Romanticize your morning commute" — beautiful cycling footage at golden hour through Valencia
- This is your cycling POV repackaged for the current trend, with music-forward editing and no narration
- Key: trending audio is mandatory. Use whatever euphoric sound is charting that week.

### Trend 4: Carousels/Photo Mode
TikTok's photo carousel format (introduced late 2025) is getting massive distribution in 2026, especially in Spain. Adapt:
- "5 cosas que encontre esta semana" — photo carousel of street finds
- "El antes y despues de esta comoda" — photo carousel transformation
- Much easier to produce than video, and currently getting algorithmic boost

## 7. BREAKING OUT OF ALGORITHMIC SUPPRESSION

Zero new followers in February is a severe signal. Here are specific tactical moves:

### Tactic 1: The "Stitch Bait" Strategy
Create content specifically designed for other creators to stitch:
- "Cual es la cosa mas loca que habeis encontrado en la calle?" (face to camera, genuine question)
- "Ensenameun mueble que encontrasteis en la basura" (challenge prompt)
- Every stitch from another creator exposes your account to their audience

### Tactic 2: Comment Section Seeding
- Go to popular Spanish home decor / DIY / minimalism creators
- Leave genuinely useful comments (NOT "check out my page")
- Example: On a video about expensive furniture, comment "En Valencia alguien tiro uno de estos la semana pasada. Lo vi desde la bici."
- Goal: curiosity clicks to your profile, not direct promotion

### Tactic 3: Duet Strategy
- Duet popular "room transformation" or "haul" videos with your street-find equivalent
- Split screen: them buying expensive furniture | you finding similar items for free
- The contrast is inherently engaging and shareable

### Tactic 4: Go Live
TikTok's algorithm treats Live sessions as a separate signal. Going live even once resets some algorithmic signals:
- Go live while cycling through Valencia
- "Ahora mismo buscando muebles por Ruzafa — vamos a ver que encontramos"
- Interactive: viewers suggest streets, you explore them
- Even 15-20 viewers on a live session sends positive signals

### Tactic 5: New Audio, Always
Stop using the same background music or AI narration voice. Every video should use a different trending sound. The algorithm tracks audio diversity as a freshness signal. Check TikTok's "Trending" section in the sound library before every post.

### Tactic 6: Hashtag Reset
Drop ALL your current hashtags. They may be flagged or deprioritized. Switch to:
- 2 broad hashtags (#valencia #reciclaje)
- 2 niche hashtags (#mueblesgratis #hallazgocallejero)
- 1 trending hashtag (changes weekly)
- 0 spam hashtags (no #fyp #foryou #viral — these are confirmed to hurt distribution in 2026)

## 10. WHAT NOT TO DO

1. **Do not crosspost Instagram Reels to TikTok**. TikTok detects and deprioritizes content with Instagram watermarks or aspect ratio mismatches. Create TikTok-native content.
2. **Do not post 7 times per week**. The 2026 algorithm punishes volume without variety. 3x/week with different formats beats 7x/week with the same format.
3. **Do not use the same AI narration voice for every video**. Variety in audio (trending sounds, voice-to-camera, text-only) signals freshness.
4. **Do not put CTAs in the video**. Put "Link in bio" and app mentions in comments, not the video itself. TikTok suppresses videos with explicit promotional language.
5. **Do not chase views**. Chase completion rate and shares. A video with 2K views and 50% completion rate is worth more than 10K views with 10% completion.
6. **Do not spread Upcycling Carlos across 6 platforms**. Concentrate on Pinterest + Instagram only.

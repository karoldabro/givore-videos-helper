# Givore Content Format Guide -- 18 Distinct Formats

**Date**: 2026-03-28
**Purpose**: Break content fatigue by diversifying format structure, not just hooks
**Constraint**: Solo creator, zero budget, cycling POV footage + AI narration pipeline

---

## Research Summary

**TikTok Spain 2026 algorithm priorities:**
- Watch time over views (completion rate is king)
- Format diversity actively rewarded; repetitive formats get suppressed
- "Dosis de Realidad" (authenticity), "Descubrimientos Fuera de la Ruta" (curiosity-driven discovery), "ROI Emocional" (emotional payoff) are TikTok's 3 official 2026 pillars
- Series content gets 2.8x higher return viewer rates; 3-part series = 89% retention
- Sharing is up 45% YoY -- content designed to be sent to a friend outperforms
- European creators win with "Proof of Humanity" -- stutters, bad lighting, real moments
- Mini-stories with problem-development-resolution structure are addictive
- Quiet flex aesthetic: calm, confident, intentional

**What the Givore algorithm sees today:** Same format signal every video (cycling POV + narration about item). Algorithm classifies it as one repeating format, pushes to same shrinking audience pool, reach decays.

**The fix:** Each format below sends a DIFFERENT signal to the algorithm -- different pacing, different text overlay patterns, different audio signatures, different engagement triggers. Even using the same underlying footage, the structural differences trick the algorithm into treating them as distinct content types.

## FORMAT 1: "El Ranking Callejero"

**Name**: El Ranking Callejero
**Pillar**: Cycling POV + Hidden Spots
**Length**: 45-60 seconds
**Viral potential**: HIGH
**Production difficulty**: LOW

**Hook (first 3 seconds):**
"Ranking de los hallazgos mas locos de esta semana -- del peor al mejor."

**Structure (second by second):**
- 0-3s: Face or text overlay with ranking title. Bold text: "RANKING SEMANAL". Quick cut energy.
- 3-8s: Item #5 (worst). Cycling approach, brief glimpse. Text overlay: "#5 -- Un cojin mojado. 1/10". Quick dismissive narration.
- 8-15s: Item #4. Same format, slightly better item. Text: "#4". Building anticipation.
- 15-22s: Item #3. Mid-tier. Narration gets more interested.
- 22-32s: Item #2. Good find. Slow down the pacing. Narration shows genuine excitement.
- 32-45s: Item #1 (best). Extended reveal. Multiple angles. Narration peaks. Text overlay: "HALLAZGO DE LA SEMANA".
- 45-55s: Quick recap montage of all 5 with scores flashing.
- 55-60s: "Que nota le poneis vosotros? Comentad." (CTA in narration, not text)

**Why it works:**
- Ranking format is inherently completable (people MUST see #1)
- Comment bait: "I disagree with your ranking" drives engagement
- Shareable: people send rankings to friends
- Each week is a new episode (series format = 2.8x return viewers)
- Algorithm reads: list format + text overlays + engagement trigger = different signal from standard narration

**Production notes:**
- Batch from existing footage: pick 5 clips from different rides
- Text overlays added in Kdenlive (template-able)
- Narration: short, punchy sentences per item
- Music: upbeat, building energy (trending sound if possible)
- Can prepare a week of footage, script in 20 min

**Example caption:**
"Ranking semanal de hallazgos en Valencia. El #1 os va a flipar. Que nota le poneis? #valencia #hallazgos #ranking #ciclismourbano #enbici"

## FORMAT 2: "60 Segundos en..."

**Name**: 60 Segundos en [Barrio]
**Pillar**: Barrio Guides + Cycling POV
**Length**: 55-65 seconds (strict timer feel)
**Viral potential**: HIGH
**Production difficulty**: LOW

**Hook (first 3 seconds):**
"60 segundos para enseñaros Benimaclet. Empiezo YA." (timer sound effect)

**Structure (second by second):**
- 0-3s: Text overlay "60 SEGUNDOS EN BENIMACLET" with countdown timer graphic. Urgency in voice.
- 3-12s: "Lo primero que veis" -- cycling entry shot into the barrio. 2-3 quick visual landmarks.
- 12-22s: "Lo que NO sale en las guias" -- hidden corner, unusual detail, something locals know.
- 22-32s: "Lo mejor para comer" -- quick cycling-by of a specific spot (market stall, bakery, bar).
- 32-42s: "El dato que nadie os cuenta" -- one surprising fact or local character observation.
- 42-52s: "Y lo que encontrais en la calle" -- quick street-find or interesting discard (Givore connection, natural).
- 52-60s: "Eso es Benimaclet en 60 segundos. Que barrio hago manana?" Timer hits zero.

**Why it works:**
- Time constraint creates urgency (100% completion rate boost)
- "What barrio next?" drives comments = algorithm fuel
- 87 barrios in Valencia = 87 episodes minimum
- Local pride = shares (people tag friends from that barrio)
- Expandable to other cities (Madrid, Barcelona, Sevilla) for massive keyword reach
- Sends DIFFERENT signal: timer format + rapid cuts + geographic tags

**Production notes:**
- Need 5-6 clips per barrio (10-second each), all cycling POV
- One afternoon of cycling = 3-4 barrio episodes
- Timer graphic: reusable Kdenlive template
- Narration: fast-paced, energetic, 120-130 words
- SFX: clock ticking underneath narration at low volume

**Example caption:**
"Benimaclet en 60 segundos. Os lo resumo TODO. Que barrio hago manana? Pedidlo en comentarios. #benimaclet #valencia #barriosdevalencia #enbici #60segundos"

## FORMAT 3: "Lo Que Nadie Ve"

**Name**: Lo Que Nadie Ve (Desde la Bici)
**Pillar**: Hidden Spots + Daily Life
**Length**: 30-40 seconds
**Viral potential**: HIGH
**Production difficulty**: VERY LOW

**Hook (first 3 seconds):**
"Esto solo lo ves si vas en bici por Valencia." (mysterious/intriguing tone)

**Structure (second by second):**
- 0-3s: Black screen or blurred shot. Text: "LO QUE NADIE VE". Voice low, conspiratorial.
- 3-10s: First reveal. Something you only notice at bike speed/height: a rooftop garden, a cat colony, a hidden courtyard through an open gate, a street artist working.
- 10-18s: Second reveal. Different type of hidden thing. Narration: "Y si girais aqui..."
- 18-26s: Third reveal. The best one. Longer shot. Let the visual breathe.
- 26-35s: Brief philosophical reflection: "La ciudad tiene capas. Solo las ves si vas despacio." (or similar -- varies each episode)
- 35-40s: Cycling away shot. No CTA in video. CTA in comments only.

**Why it works:**
- Mystery/reveal format has highest share-to-view ratio on TikTok
- "Only if you bike" creates an exclusive in-group feeling
- Short length = high completion rate = algorithm boost
- Calm, contemplative tone = "quiet flex" aesthetic trending in 2026
- Zero Givore mention = pure growth content (70% of content mix)
- Sends DIFFERENT signal: slow pacing + ambient audio + contemplative mood

**Production notes:**
- Pull from ANY cycling footage -- look for moments of unexpected beauty
- Minimal narration (50-70 words max)
- Music: lo-fi or ambient, NOT the usual upbeat track
- No text overlays except the opening title
- Natural audio underneath: bike chain, wind, city sounds

**Example caption:**
"Lo que solo ves si vas en bici. Valencia tiene capas que no veis desde el coche. #valencia #ciclismourbano #lugaressecretos #loquenadieve #enbici"

## FORMAT 4: "Bici vs Coche"

**Name**: Bici vs Coche
**Pillar**: Cycling POV + City Comparisons
**Length**: 35-50 seconds
**Viral potential**: VERY HIGH (debate bait)
**Production difficulty**: LOW

**Hook (first 3 seconds):**
"Lo que ves en coche vs lo que veo yo en bici por la misma calle." (split-screen implication)

**Structure (second by second):**
- 0-3s: Split text overlay: "COCHE | BICI". Strong visual contrast setup.
- 3-10s: "En coche: atascos, semaforos, el mismo camino de siempre." Footage: static, boring street-level perspective (can simulate with slow footage or stills).
- 10-20s: "En bici: [specific beautiful/interesting thing]." Your actual cycling footage showing the discovery angle. Sharp contrast.
- 20-28s: Second comparison pair. "En coche: aparcas a 3 calles. En bici: llegas a la puerta."
- 28-36s: Third comparison. "En coche: no ves esto." -- reveal the hidden thing (mural, street find, cat, beautiful corner).
- 36-45s: "Eso si, en coche no te llueve encima." Self-deprecating humor. Quick rain clip if available.
- 45-50s: "Cada uno elige. Yo ya elegi." Cycling-away shot.

**Why it works:**
- Comparison format is TikTok's most debate-generating structure
- "Team bici" vs "team coche" splits the comments = massive engagement
- Self-deprecating humor prevents it from feeling preachy
- Shareable: cyclists send to car friends and vice versa
- Touches sustainability without being heavy-handed
- Sends DIFFERENT signal: comparison structure + debate trigger + humor

**Production notes:**
- "Car perspective" can be simulated: static shots, Google Street View stills, or just text descriptions
- Cycling footage: pick your most visually striking clips
- Split-screen effect in Kdenlive or just alternate cuts with text labels
- Narration: playful, not aggressive. Never lecture.
- End with self-deprecation to disarm critics

**Example caption:**
"Bici vs coche por la misma calle de Valencia. Team bici o team coche? No vale decir patinete. #bicivscoche #ciclismourbano #valencia #movilidadsostenible #enbici"

## FORMAT 5: "El Iceberg de Valencia"

**Name**: El Iceberg de [Ciudad/Tema]
**Pillar**: Hidden Spots + Barrio Guides
**Length**: 60-90 seconds
**Viral potential**: HIGH
**Production difficulty**: MEDIUM

**Hook (first 3 seconds):**
"El iceberg de Valencia en bici. Cuanto mas abajo, menos gente lo sabe."

**Structure (second by second):**
- 0-3s: Iceberg graphic (simple, reusable template). Text overlay levels. Eerie/intriguing music.
- 3-15s: SURFACE LEVEL: "Lo que todo el mundo conoce." Ciudad de las Artes, Turia, playa Malvarrosa. Quick clips, fast cuts. Known landmarks cycling-by.
- 15-30s: MID LEVEL: "Lo que saben los que viven aqui." Specific barrio secrets -- the bakery in Ruzafa, the cat alley in El Carmen, the hidden plaza. Slower, more detailed shots.
- 30-50s: DEEP LEVEL: "Lo que solo ves si vas en bici." Ultra-specific observations -- the house with 40 plants, the abuela who waves every morning, the doorway that opens to a courtyard. Intimate cycling footage.
- 50-70s: ABYSS LEVEL: "Y esto... no se lo cuento a nadie." The weirdest, most surprising, or most beautiful secret. Build maximum anticipation. Long reveal.
- 70-80s: "Hay mas niveles. Parte 2?" -- series hook.
- 80-90s: Quick scroll through the iceberg graphic again. "Comentad en que nivel estais."

**Why it works:**
- Iceberg format has proven virality across all niches
- Inherently completable: must reach the bottom
- "What level am I?" drives comment engagement
- Series-able: by theme (food iceberg, barrio iceberg, street finds iceberg)
- Comment section becomes a community sharing their own "deep level" finds
- Sends DIFFERENT signal: graphic-heavy + layered information + series hook

**Production notes:**
- Iceberg graphic: create ONE template in Kdenlive, reuse with different text
- Need diverse clip library sorted by "how well-known" the location is
- Longer format (60-90s) -- watch time matters more than completion rate at this length
- Music: mysterious/building, NOT cycling-upbeat
- Can make icebergs about: barrios, street food, cycling routes, street finds

**Example caption:**
"El iceberg de Valencia en bici. En que nivel estais? Si llegais al fondo sois de verdad de aqui. Parte 2 si llegamos a 500 likes. #iceberg #valencia #secretosdevalencia #lugaressecretos #enbici"

## FORMAT 6: "Sonidos de la Calle"

**Name**: Sonidos de [Barrio/Ciudad]
**Pillar**: City Sounds / ASMR
**Length**: 25-40 seconds
**Viral potential**: MEDIUM-HIGH
**Production difficulty**: VERY LOW

**Hook (first 3 seconds):**
NO NARRATION. Pure sound. Text overlay: "SONIDOS DE RUZAFA -- 8:00 AM". Bike chain clicking.

**Structure (second by second):**
- 0-3s: Black screen fades in. Only ambient sound. Text: location + time. Immediate pattern break (no voice = curiosity).
- 3-10s: First sound scene. Birds + distant church bell + bike wheels on cobblestone. Slow cycling POV.
- 10-18s: Second sound scene. Market opening: metal shutters, vendors calling, crates stacking. Text overlay identifying each sound.
- 18-26s: Third sound scene. The unexpected one: a parrot on a balcony, flamenco from an open window, kids running to school.
- 26-35s: Sounds blend together. Wider cycling shot showing the full street alive.
- 35-40s: Fade to black. Text: "Cada barrio suena diferente. Escuchad."

**Why it works:**
- ASMR/ambient content: 12%+ engagement rate (above platform average of 8%)
- Total pattern break from narrated content = algorithm sees NEW format
- No narration = works in ANY language = potential for international reach
- Calming content gets saved (saves = powerful algorithm signal in 2026)
- "Sustainability ASMR" is an emerging 2026 trend
- People listen with headphones = higher attention quality

**Production notes:**
- ZERO narration needed (saves pipeline time)
- Requires: cycling footage with GOOD ambient audio (not wind-blasted)
- If audio quality is poor: layer city ambience from free sound libraries over footage
- Text overlays only (identifying sounds)
- Music: NONE. Pure ambient sound is the format.
- One ride through a barrio in the morning = enough for 2-3 episodes

**Example caption:**
"Los sonidos de Ruzafa a las 8 de la manana. Cerrad los ojos y escuchad vuestra ciudad. Que barrio suena mejor? #asmr #sonidos #ruzafa #valencia #soundscape #ciclismourbano"

## FORMAT 7: "Pregunta del Dia"

**Name**: Pregunta del Dia (Pedaleando)
**Pillar**: Daily Life + Community
**Length**: 20-30 seconds (micro-format)
**Viral potential**: MEDIUM (but HIGH for follower conversion)
**Production difficulty**: VERY LOW

**Hook (first 3 seconds):**
"Pregunta seria mientras pedaleo: [provocative question]?" Text overlay with the question.

**Structure (second by second):**
- 0-3s: Cycling POV. Big text overlay with the question. Voice reads it out.
- 3-12s: Brief personal take (NOT a lecture -- just an opinion). Keep cycling. "Yo personalmente..."
- 12-20s: "Pero es que ayer vi [specific thing that prompted the question]." Show the footage.
- 20-28s: "No se, decidme vosotros. Estoy flipando con lo que opina la gente." (Do NOT answer definitively)
- 28-30s: End on the cycling footage. Question appears as text again.

**Question examples:**
- "Es normal dejar un mueble perfecto en la calle?"
- "Valencia tiene los mejores carriles bici de Espana o me lo invento?"
- "Cuando veis un sofa en la acera, que pensais primero?"
- "En que barrio vivirias con 800 euros al mes?"
- "Meter la bici en el metro deberia ser gratis?"

**Why it works:**
- Micro-format = nearly 100% completion rate
- Question = MUST comment = engagement bomb
- Duet/stitch bait: other creators respond with their take
- Daily-able: one question per day requires minimal production
- Builds community feeling: "this creator asks what I think"
- Follower conversion: people follow to see the next question and the comments

**Production notes:**
- Uses throwaway cycling footage (any ride works)
- 40-60 words of narration only
- Text overlay of the question is mandatory (sound-off viewers)
- No music, just natural cycling sounds
- Batch-scriptable: write 7 questions in 10 minutes

**Example caption:**
"Pregunta del dia. De verdad quiero saber. Responded en comentarios, las mejores respuestas las leo en el video de manana. #pregunta #valencia #ciclismourbano #debate #enbici"

## FORMAT 8: "Timelapse Rutero"

**Name**: Ruta Completa en [X] Segundos
**Pillar**: Cycling POV (pure)
**Length**: 30-45 seconds
**Viral potential**: MEDIUM-HIGH
**Production difficulty**: MEDIUM

**Hook (first 3 seconds):**
"Mi ruta diaria: 7 kilometros en 30 segundos." Map animation or route overlay.

**Structure (second by second):**
- 0-3s: Map showing the route (simple graphic or Google Maps screenshot with drawn line). Text: distance + time compressed.
- 3-8s: Start point. Normal speed for 2 seconds, then ACCELERATE to 8-16x timelapse.
- 8-25s: The route in hyperlapse. Key landmarks get a brief text callout as they flash by. Music drives the energy.
- 25-30s: Arrival. DECELERATE back to normal speed at destination. Satisfying stop.
- 30-38s: "7 km. 25 minutos. 0 euros en gasolina. 0 minutos buscando aparcamiento."
- 38-45s: Reverse angle or look-back. "Manana la misma ruta, diferente hallazgo."

**Why it works:**
- Hyperlapse is visually hypnotic (high watch time, replays)
- Route/map content is highly saveable (saves = algorithm gold)
- Practical value for other cyclists (keyword: "rutas bici Valencia" = 1.6K monthly)
- Satisfying speed transitions trigger dopamine
- Different city routes = infinite episodes
- NO narration needed during timelapse section (just music) = different audio signal

**Production notes:**
- Requires: long continuous cycling footage (one full ride)
- Speed up in editing (8x-16x in Kdenlive)
- Map graphic: screenshot Google Maps, draw route in GIMP, 2 minutes
- Brief narration at start and end only
- Music: MUST be energetic, electronic, driving beat
- Process one long ride into timelapse = 30 min editing max

**Example caption:**
"Mi ruta diaria por Valencia en 30 segundos. 7 km, 25 min, y siempre encuentro algo nuevo. Cual es vuestra ruta? #timelapse #ciclismourbano #rutasbici #valencia #enbici #hiperlapse"

## FORMAT 9: "No Toqueis Eso"

**Name**: No Toqueis Eso (Street Finds Educational)
**Pillar**: Minimalism / Decluttering + Street Finds
**Length**: 35-50 seconds
**Viral potential**: HIGH (controversy + education)
**Production difficulty**: LOW

**Hook (first 3 seconds):**
"Veis ese sofa en la calle? NO lo cojais. Os explico." (pattern interrupt: you EXPECT them to say grab it)

**Structure (second by second):**
- 0-3s: Cycling approach to a street item. Dramatic stop or slow-down. Text: "NO LO COJAIS". Contrarian hook.
- 3-12s: "Primer error: [specific reason]." E.g., water damage signs, bed bug risk, structural issue. Educational. Specific. Zoomed-in footage.
- 12-22s: "Segundo: [practical consideration]." Transport logistics, cleaning needs, whether it is actually worth saving.
- 22-32s: "PERO... si veis ESTO" -- pivot to a GOOD find nearby or criteria for what IS worth grabbing. Positive turn.
- 32-42s: "La diferencia entre basura y tesoro: [specific criteria]." Quick educational content.
- 42-50s: "Ahora ya sabeis. La proxima vez que veais algo en la calle, fijaos en esto." Cycling away.

**Why it works:**
- Contrarian hook: "DON'T take it" is the opposite of what the audience expects
- Educational content gets saved AND shared
- Positions creator as expert (authority = trust = follows)
- Addresses real concerns (bed bugs, hygiene) that commenters always raise
- Natural Givore connection without forcing it
- Comment debate: "I would have taken it!" = engagement

**Production notes:**
- Use footage of items you actually found problematic
- Narration needs to sound knowledgeable, not preachy
- Close-up shots of specific damage/issues (zoom in Kdenlive)
- Can batch: film 3-4 "no touch" items in one ride
- Music: slightly tense/dramatic for the warning, then lighter for the pivot

**Example caption:**
"No todo lo de la calle merece segunda vida. Aprended a distinguir tesoro de trampa. Que es lo peor que habeis cogido de la calle? #streetfinds #hallazgos #reciclaje #muebles #valencia #consejosutiles"

## FORMAT 10: "POV: Eres Mi Bici"

**Name**: POV: Eres Mi Bici
**Pillar**: Cycling POV + Daily Life (humorous angle)
**Length**: 25-35 seconds
**Viral potential**: HIGH (POV format is evergreen)
**Production difficulty**: LOW

**Hook (first 3 seconds):**
Text overlay: "POV: eres mi bici y hoy me toca ruta por El Carmen". No voice. Just cycling footage starting. Then inner-monologue narration BEGINS from the bike's perspective.

**Structure (second by second):**
- 0-3s: Standard cycling start. Text overlay sets the POV premise. Funny music or quirky sound.
- 3-10s: Bike's inner monologue: "Venga, otro dia. A ver que encuentra este hoy en la calle." Resigned, sarcastic tone.
- 10-18s: Dramatic moment: pothole, near-miss, cobblestones. Bike reacts: "AY. MIS RUEDAS. Otra vez por aqui no, por favor."
- 18-25s: Discovery of something interesting. Bike's take: "No. No no no. No vamos a parar. No cabe. PARA. No para--" (creator stops).
- 25-30s: Bike sighs: "Otra mesa. Genial. Mis radios no dan para mas."
- 30-35s: "Hasta manana. Si sobrevivo." End.

**Why it works:**
- POV format remains TikTok's most adaptable viral format
- Anthropomorphizing objects = highly shareable (people send it saying "this is literally us")
- Humor + personality = follows
- Each ride can have a different "mood" for the bike (tired, excited, scared, philosophical)
- Completely different AUDIO signal from standard narration (character voice, humor, sound effects)
- Series potential: "POV: eres mi bici" becomes a recurring character

**Production notes:**
- Same cycling footage, COMPLETELY different narration treatment
- Voice: needs slight character shift (sarcastic, tired, dramatic) from normal narration
- ElevenLabs: could use same voice but adjust stability/style for comedic delivery
- Short format = fast production
- Subtitles are critical (the humor is in the text)

**Example caption:**
"Mi bici ya no me aguanta. Si pudiera hablar, diria esto. POV eres mi bici. #pov #bici #humor #valencia #ciclismourbano #comedia"

## FORMAT 11: "Antes Aqui Habia..."

**Name**: Antes Aqui Habia...
**Pillar**: Barrio Guides + Hidden Spots + Community
**Length**: 40-55 seconds
**Viral potential**: HIGH (nostalgia + debate)
**Production difficulty**: MEDIUM

**Hook (first 3 seconds):**
"Antes aqui habia un bar que conocia todo el barrio. Ahora hay esto." (cycling past a gentrified spot)

**Structure (second by second):**
- 0-3s: Cycling past a specific location. Voice with nostalgic tone. Text overlay: "ANTES AQUI HABIA..."
- 3-12s: What WAS there. Narrate the memory or the local knowledge. Old photo if available (optional -- even description works).
- 12-22s: What is there NOW. Show it cycling past. Let the visual speak.
- 22-30s: Second location. Same format: before/after. Faster pacing now.
- 30-40s: Third location. The most impactful change.
- 40-48s: Brief reflection. NOT preachy. "No digo que sea malo. Solo digo que era diferente." Balanced.
- 48-55s: "Que ha cambiado en VUESTRO barrio? Contadme." Cycling away.

**Why it works:**
- Nostalgia is one of TikTok's most powerful emotional triggers
- LOCAL nostalgia drives explosive sharing ("tag someone who remembers")
- Debate content (gentrification) = comments = algorithm fuel
- Older demographic (your 35-44 core) connects deeply with "what changed"
- Each barrio has dozens of "before" stories = endless content
- Stitch-able: locals add their own "before there was..."

**Production notes:**
- Research: ask locals, check Google Maps history, old photos
- Current footage: cycling past the location in question
- Old photos: optional but powerful (search local Valencia forums, Instagram archives)
- Narration: warm, not angry. Observational, not political.
- Music: slightly melancholic, nostalgic Spanish guitar or lo-fi

**Example caption:**
"Las calles cambian. Los recuerdos no. Que habia antes en VUESTRA calle que ya no esta? Contadme en comentarios. #valencia #nostalgia #barrios #antesydespues #gentrificacion"

**Viral potential**: MEDIUM-HIGH
**Production difficulty**: VERY LOW

"Lo mas raro que he visto hoy en bici por Valencia." Immediate curiosity gap.

- 3-8s: Build-up. Cycling approach to the weird thing. "Iba por [street/barrio] y de repente..."

## FORMAT 13: "3 Cosas Que No Sabiais"

**Name**: 3 Cosas Que No Sabiais de [Barrio/Tema]
**Pillar**: Barrio Guides + Hidden Spots (educational)
**Length**: 40-55 seconds
**Viral potential**: MEDIUM-HIGH
**Production difficulty**: LOW

**Hook (first 3 seconds):**
"3 cosas que no sabiais de Ruzafa. La tercera os va a flipar." Classic listicle hook with third-item tease.

**Structure (second by second):**
- 0-3s: Cycling into the barrio. Bold text: "3 COSAS -- RUZAFA". Number "1" appears.
- 3-15s: Fact #1. Interesting but not mind-blowing. Cycling footage showing the relevant spot. Clear narration of the fact. Text overlay with key phrase.
- 15-28s: Fact #2. More surprising. Pace picks up. "Y esto tampoco lo sabiais..."
- 28-42s: Fact #3. The promised mind-blower. Slow build. Extended reveal. Visual proof.
- 42-50s: "Cual os ha sorprendido mas? La 1, la 2 o la 3?" Text overlay with the three options.
- 50-55s: "Manana: 3 cosas de [next barrio]. Seguidme para no perderos nada."

**Why it works:**
- Listicle + "the third one" tease = proven completion driver
- Educational = saves (the most powerful 2026 algorithm signal)
- Numbered content = clear structure = viewer stays oriented
- Vote CTA ("1, 2 or 3?") is low-friction high-engagement
- Series format: each barrio, each theme = episodes
- Micro-masterclass trend: depth builds loyalty

**Production notes:**
- Research: 10 min per barrio (Wikipedia, local blogs, personal knowledge)
- Footage: cycling through the barrio, showing relevant locations
- 3 text overlays per video (one per fact)
- Narration: informative but conversational, not Wikipedia-reading

## ALGORITHM SIGNALS COMPARED

The entire point of format diversity is that each format sends DIFFERENT signals to TikTok's classifier:

| Signal | Old Format (all videos) | New Formats (varied) |
|--------|------------------------|---------------------|
| Audio pattern | Narration + music (same) | Narration / silence / ASMR / character voice / music-only |
| Pacing | Medium (same) | Ultra-fast / slow / timelapse / micro |
| Text overlays | Minimal (same) | Rankings / timers / maps / questions / none |
| Engagement trigger | Generic CTA | Debate / vote / guess / comment location / request barrio |
| Video length | 40-60s (same) | 15s to 90s range |
| Mood | Informative (same) | Funny / contemplative / nostalgic / mysterious / educational |

Each format opens a DIFFERENT distribution pathway in the algorithm, reaching audience segments the old single format was never shown to.
